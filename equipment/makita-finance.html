MAKITA FINANCE FAQ

Common questions from tradesmen

Can I finance Makita 18V LXT cordless tools and combo kits?

Yes. We finance all Makita 18V LXT cordless systems including drills, impact drivers, circular saws, grinders, multitools and complete combo kits (2-10 piece sets). The legendary teal tools trusted by professionals. Flexible 1-5 year terms available.

What are the monthly payments for a Makita combo kit?

A Makita 2-piece combo kit (drill + impact driver, £300-£400) costs from £25-£30/month over 2 years. A 6-piece kit (£800-£1,200) is from £60-£90/month. Professional 10-piece kits (£2,000-£3,000) are from £145-£220/month over 2 years.

Do you finance Makita cordless saws and grinders?

Absolutely. We finance all Makita cordless tools including circular saws (DHS680, DHS710), mitre saws (DLS714), angle grinders (DGA504, DGA511), reciprocating saws and track saws. Individual tools or complete kit packages. Essential for carpenters, electricians and builders.

Is Makita better than DeWalt or Milwaukee for UK tradesmen?

All three are premium brands. Makita's 18V LXT system has the largest cordless tool range (200+ tools), excellent battery life and competitive pricing. Many UK tradesmen prefer Makita for reliability and value. DeWalt and Milwaukee are also excellent. We finance all three brands.

Can I add Makita batteries and chargers to my finance agreement?

Yes. Smart move. Finance complete Makita systems including tools + spare batteries (3.0Ah, 5.0Ah, 6.0Ah) + multi-port chargers + storage cases. This gives you maximum uptime on site and one monthly payment. We recommend 4-6 batteries for full-time professional use.

Do I need to be a limited company to finance Makita tools?

No. We work with sole traders, partnerships, LLPs and limited companies. Whether you're a self-employed carpenter, electrician or building contractor, if you've been trading 6+ months and have regular income, you can apply for Makita finance.
