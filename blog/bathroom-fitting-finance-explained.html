Bathroom fitting is lucrative work with healthy margins - when you've got the kit to do it properly. WHAT BATHROOM FITTERS ACTUALLY NEED TO INVEST

Bathroom fitting sits somewhere between plumbing and building work. You need plumbing skills, tiling ability, basic carpentry, and enough electrical knowledge to safely install lighting and showers. That translates to diverse equipment needs.

BATHROOM FITTER FINANCE OPTIONS

You're not stuck with one option. Different finance products suit different situations. Here's what actually works for bathroom fitters:

Equipment Finance / Hire Purchase

Perfect for big-ticket items: vans, expensive diagnostic equipment, full tool packages.

How it works:

  • You choose equipment from any UK supplier
  • Finance company pays supplier directly
  • You pay fixed monthly instalments (36-60 months typical)
  • You own equipment after final payment
  • Deposit: 10-20% typical (sometimes 0%)

APR rates: 6.9-12.9% depending on credit history and business age

Example: £15,000 equipment package over 60 months = £310/month at 8.9% APR

Business Loans (Unsecured)

Gives you cash to spend how you need. Perfect for mixed purchases: some tools, some stock, some marketing, bit of working capital.

Typical terms:

  • £5,000-£50,000 available
  • 12-60 month repayment terms
  • APR: 9-16% (higher than secured finance)
  • No equipment security required
  • Cash in account within 3-5 days

Best for: Bathroom fitters who need flexibility or are buying from multiple suppliers (Screwfix, Plumb Center, toolshops, private sellers).

Invoice Finance - The Cash Flow Saver

Bathroom projects often involve 30-60 day payment terms from builders or homeowners. Invoice finance releases 80-90% of your invoice value within 24 hours.

How it works:

  • You complete bathroom installation, raise invoice
  • Finance company advances 80-90% immediately
  • You get remaining balance when customer pays
  • Fee: 1.5-3% of invoice value

Example: £5,000 bathroom job completed. You get £4,250 within 24 hours. Customer pays in 45 days. You receive remaining £600 (£750 minus £150 fee).

Perfect for: Bathroom fitters working with property developers, housing associations, or commercial clients with extended payment terms.

Van Finance

Your van is your workshop, your storage, and your business card. Finance it separately or bundle it with equipment.

Typical van finance for bathroom fitters:

  • Vehicle value: £8,000-£25,000
  • New or up to 8 years old
  • Deposit: 10-30% (lower for newer vans)
  • Term: 36-60 months
  • APR: 5.9-10.9% (better rates than personal loans)

Example: £12,000 Ford Transit Custom (3 years old), £2,000 deposit, 48 months = £245/month

WHAT LENDERS LOOK AT FOR BATHROOM FITTERS

Finance approval isn't mysterious. Here's exactly what they check:

1. Trading History

Most specialist lenders want 12 months minimum. Some will consider 6 months if cash flow is strong and you've got upcoming work booked. High street banks want 2-3 years - ignore them unless you've been trading ages.

2. Cash Flow

Can you afford the monthly payment? Lenders typically want to see 2-3x the monthly finance cost in regular income.

Quick math: £400/month finance = need £800-£1,200/month minimum turnover after other business expenses.

3. Credit History

Personal and business credit both checked. One or two missed payments won't destroy your application. Active CCJs or defaults under 3 years make things harder (not impossible).

4. Insurance & Qualifications

Public liability insurance (£2-5M cover) is standard. Gas Safe registration if doing any gas work. Not always required for finance approval, but helps demonstrate you're legitimate.

COMMON QUESTIONS FROM BATHROOM FITTERS

Can I finance stock/materials as well as tools?

Yes, but there are limits. Initial stock (fittings, pipes, sundries) up to £3,000-£5,000 can usually be included in equipment finance. For ongoing stock/materials purchases, consider invoice finance or a business overdraft instead.

What if I'm subcontracting to builders?

That's fine. Lenders care about consistent income, not who pays you. If you're regularly invoicing builders or main contractors, you're good to go. Invoice finance works particularly well for this scenario.

I've only been trading 8 months - can I still get finance?

Possibly. Some lenders will consider 6-9 months trading if cash flow is strong. You'll likely need a larger deposit (20-30%) and rates might be slightly higher. Worth applying to find out.

Can I add my partner/employee to the van insurance?

Yes. Van finance doesn't restrict who can drive. You arrange your own insurance separately (fully comp is usually required by finance company).
