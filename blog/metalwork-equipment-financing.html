METALWORK EQUIPMENT FINANCING GUIDE

Published: 13 January 2025 | Reading time: 8 mins

Metal fabrication isn't cheap. A professional MIG welder costs what most people earn in a month. A plasma cutter costs what they earn in six. And a fully-equipped workshop? That's a house deposit. But here's the thing: you can't win commercial contracts without proper kit. This is the straight-talking guide to financing metalwork equipment without bankrupting yourself.

THE METALWORK EQUIPMENT YOU ACTUALLY NEED

Let's be clear about what professional metalwork actually costs. This isn't hobbyist gear from Screwfix - this is the machinery that separates weekend warriors from legitimate fabrication businesses.

Welding Equipment (£2,000-£15,000)

Your bread and butter. Every metal fabricator needs at least one professional welder, and most need three different types for versatility.

MIG Welders (£2,000-£6,000):

  • Entry-level professional: £2,000-£3,500 (180-250 amps)
  • Mid-range industrial: £4,000-£6,000 (300-400 amps)
  • Heavy-duty commercial: £7,000+ (450+ amps, synergic control)
  • Typical choice: Lincoln Electric or Miller at £3,500 - finance at £68/month over 60 months

TIG Welders (£3,000-£12,000):

  • Essential for stainless steel, aluminum, precision work
  • AC/DC models start at £3,000
  • Professional units with pulse control: £6,000-£8,000
  • High-frequency start, remote control: £8,000-£12,000
  • Popular choice: Fronius or ESAB at £7,500 - finance at £147/month over 60 months

Arc/Stick Welders (£1,500-£4,000):

  • Perfect for site work and structural steel
  • Portable units: £1,500-£2,500
  • Workshop inverters: £2,500-£4,000
  • Most fabricators finance at £45-£80/month

Plasma Cutters (£3,000-£25,000)

Game-changer for any fabrication business. Cut through 25mm steel like butter, or slice intricate patterns in 2mm sheet. But quality costs.

Handheld Plasma Systems:

  • Entry-level (40-60 amps): £3,000-£5,000 - cuts up to 12mm
  • Mid-range (80-100 amps): £6,000-£10,000 - cuts up to 25mm
  • Industrial (120+ amps): £12,000-£18,000 - cuts up to 40mm
  • Hypertherm or Kjellberg at £8,500 - finance at £167/month over 60 months

CNC Plasma Tables (£20,000-£150,000):

  • Entry CNC tables: £20,000-£35,000 (1.5m x 3m bed)
  • Mid-range automated: £45,000-£80,000 (2m x 6m, downdraft)
  • Industrial systems: £100,000-£150,000 (full automation, bevel cutting)
  • Typical £45,000 system - finance at £880/month over 60 months

Press Brakes & Guillotines (£10,000-£80,000)

If you're doing gates, railings, architectural metalwork, or any precision bending, these are essential.

Press Brakes:

  • Manual hydraulic (2m): £10,000-£18,000
  • Semi-automatic (3m): £22,000-£35,000
  • CNC controlled (3m+): £45,000-£80,000
  • Most popular: 3m hydraulic at £28,000 - finance at £548/month over 60 months

Guillotines:

  • Mechanical (2.5m): £8,000-£15,000
  • Hydraulic (3m): £16,000-£28,000
  • CNC programmable: £30,000-£50,000
  • Solid choice at £18,000 - finance at £352/month over 60 months

Lathes & Mills (£8,000-£60,000)

For precision engineering, custom parts, and machining work. Not every fabricator needs these, but if you're doing bespoke work they're goldmine generators.

  • Manual centre lathe: £8,000-£15,000
  • CNC lathe: £25,000-£45,000
  • Manual milling machine: £6,000-£12,000
  • CNC mill: £30,000-£60,000+
  • Full manual setup (lathe + mill): £20,000 - finance at £391/month over 60 months

Workshop Infrastructure (£5,000-£25,000)

The stuff nobody talks about but everybody needs.

  • Fume extraction system: £3,000-£8,000 (legally required in most workshops)
  • Heavy-duty fabrication benches: £1,500-£3,000 each
  • Overhead crane/gantry: £4,000-£12,000
  • Welding screens and safety equipment: £1,000-£2,500
  • Compressor and air tools: £2,000-£5,000
  • Angle grinders, hand tools, consumables: £2,000-£4,000

METALWORK FINANCE OPTIONS EXPLAINED

Right, you've worked out what equipment you need. Now let's talk about paying for it without remortgaging your house.

Asset Finance (Hire Purchase) - The Smart Choice

This is the most popular option for metalwork equipment, and for good reason. Simple, predictable, and you own the kit at the end.

How it works:

  • You choose your welders, plasma cutters, whatever you need
  • We finance 80-100% of the cost (most lenders want 10-20% deposit)
  • Fixed monthly payments over 36-72 months (5-6 years typical)
  • Equipment is security for the loan - nothing else needed
  • You own it outright when final payment's made

Example: £42,000 equipment package (3 welders, plasma cutter, press brake) over 60 months = £822/month. Win one decent gates contract per month at £3,500 and you're £2,678/month better off.

Equipment Lease - For Regular Upgraders

Leasing means you never own the equipment - you're essentially renting it long-term. But monthly payments are 20-30% lower and it's fully tax-deductible.

Best for: Fabricators who want latest CNC technology and plan to upgrade every 4-5 years. Popular with architectural metalwork firms who need cutting-edge equipment to win contracts.

Example: £60,000 CNC plasma table on 60-month lease = £950/month vs £1,174/month HP. Saving £224/month adds up to £13,440 over the term.

Refinancing Old Equipment

Got equipment you already own? You can refinance it to raise cash for new machinery or working capital.

If you've got a £35,000 press brake that's paid off, you can typically release 70-80% of its current value (£24,500-£28,000) and use that cash to buy a plasma cutter, upgrade your welder, or cover a rough month.

Package Deals - Multiple Machines, One Payment

Smart fabricators bundle multiple equipment purchases into one finance agreement. Better rates, simpler admin, one monthly payment.

Popular packages:

  • Startup package (£18,000): MIG + TIG welder, portable plasma, extraction - £352/month
  • Mid-range package (£42,000): 3 welders, plasma cutter, press brake - £822/month
  • Full workshop (£85,000): Complete welding setup, CNC plasma, press brake, guillotine, extraction - £1,663/month

WHAT LENDERS ACTUALLY LOOK AT

Metalwork is seen as moderate-risk by lenders (skilled trade, good profit margins, strong demand). Here's what actually matters:

Trading History

Most specialist lenders want 12 months minimum trading. Some will consider 6-9 months if you've got strong orders or a decent deposit. High street banks want 2-3 years - they're usually not worth bothering with for equipment under £50,000.

Qualifications and Experience

Welding qualifications (coded welder certificates, fabrication diplomas) help but aren't always mandatory. What matters more is demonstrable experience - portfolio of work, client testimonials, order book.

Credit History

Personal and business credit both checked. One or two late payments won't kill you. CCJs or bankruptcies under 3 years old make things harder but not impossible - we've secured finance for metalworkers with imperfect credit.

Cash Flow and Affordability

Can you afford the monthly payment? They want 3-6 months bank statements. If you're consistently turning over £10,000+ monthly, most equipment deals under £40,000 are straightforward approvals.

Deposit (Sometimes)

10-20% deposit is typical, though 100% finance is possible for established businesses with good credit. Bigger deposits = better rates and easier approvals.

COMMON METALWORK FINANCE QUESTIONS

Can I finance second-hand welding equipment?

Yes, but with conditions. Equipment needs to be in good working order with reasonable remaining life. Most lenders want machines less than 8-10 years old. Rates are typically 1-2% higher than new equipment.

What if I'm just starting out as a metalworker?

Startups are harder but not impossible. You'll need 20-30% deposit, evidence of orders or contracts, and potentially a personal guarantee. Some lenders specialize in startup trades - see our home tradesman equipment loans guide for more.

Can I add equipment to my existing finance agreement?

Absolutely. Most fabricators come back for additional equipment as they grow. We can either set up a separate agreement or refinance everything together (sometimes gets better rates).

What happens if I can't make a payment?

Contact your lender immediately - ignoring it makes things worse. One missed payment isn't catastrophic. Most lenders will work with you on payment holidays, restructuring, or temporary arrangements if you're having short-term cash flow issues.

Can I pay off my equipment finance early?

Yes. Most agreements allow early settlement with either no penalty or a small redemption fee (typically 1-2 months interest). If you land a massive contract and want to clear the debt, you can.
